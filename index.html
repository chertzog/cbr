History

  • Founded in 1888
  • George Eastman
  • Controlled 90% of film market through the 1970s

Strategy

What has been Kodak’s strategy vis-à-vis traditional film-based imaging? What has made them so profitable over the long-term?

Strategy

  • Focused on consumable items (paper, film, chemicals)
  • Large scale production
  • Acquire technology as needed
  • Fast product development
  • Stress simplicity to customer

    • “You press the button, we do the rest"

Traditional vs Digital

What are the principal structural differences between traditional and digital photography? How did these differences foster Kodak’s decision-making? How have value creation and value appropriation changed in digital photography versus that of traditional photography?

Traditional

  • Camera, film, film processing
  • Vertically integrated
  • "The way its always been"

Digital

  • Cameras, photo printing, file storage
  • Outsourced
  • Sold digital cameras at a $60 loss to drive people to kiosks for prints

Competition

Assess Kodak’s market response to the introduction of Sony’s Mavica in 1981. Was it appropriate?

Sony Mavica

  • Instilled fear in Kodak - "Photography is dead"
  • "Imaging company, not film company" - Fisher
  • Kodak expanded into other industries

Fisher

Assess Fisher’s attempt to transform Kodak. Why did it fail? What might have developed had Palermo’s ideas been able to take hold?

Imaging company, not film company

  • Turned Kodak into Technology firm
  • Failed due to lack of film demand
  • Turned to hardware / kiosks and image based services
